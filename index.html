Web Map App of Andros Island

Introduction

Welcome to the Web GIS Application of Andros Island.

This platform provides interactive access to a wide range of spatial data and thematic maps related to the island such as:

  • Land Cover of Andros Island with Shaded Relief (Scale 1:150,000)
  • Settlements and Road Network of Andros Island (Scale 1:150,000)
  • Points of interest in the settlement of Mpatsi (Scale 1:5,000)
  • Soil Map (Ministry of Environment and Energy)
  • Elevation Map (Ministry of Environment and Energy)
  • Forests – Dominant Vegetation Type Map (Ministry of Environment and Energy)
  • Projection and Spatial Reference Details
  • Tranverse Mercator Projection
  • CGRS 1987 Reference System
General Information

Andros (Greek: Άνδρος, pronounced [ˈanðros]) is the northernmost island of the Greek Cyclades archipelago, about 10 km (6 mi) southeast of Euboea, and about 3 km (2 mi) north of Tinos. It is nearly 40 km (25 mi) long, and its greatest breadth is 16 km (10 mi). It is for the most part mountainous, with many fruitful and well-watered valleys.The municipality, which includes the island Andros and several small, uninhabited islands, has an area of 380 km2 (146.719 sq mi).The largest towns are Andros (town), Gavrio, Batsi, and Ormos Korthiou.
